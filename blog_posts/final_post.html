My Final Blog Post

How Would I Design The Course?

Personally, I feel that the first 2 sections of this course are structured very well, and would mimic the style from this semester. The textbook works well with how labs are assigned and functioned. I also felt that the course and Dr. Stewart's teaching style worked well, especially for the scope of the labs and projects of the first two sections.

Where I'd have to offer changes would be in the final section of the course. While I felt that Professor Johnson's textbook was probably one of the best ways to have learned the material for projects 1 and 2, I had a lot of difficulty in the later sections. Granted, I had a number of personal factors which did not help, but using the textbook as a primary and introductory resource was troublesome for me, in part due to the informal, sort of "learn as you go" mentality. Dr. Stewart as an in-class resource was immensely helpful throughout the year, mostly due to his ability to fill in any gaps I had from the textbook.

A feature of this class I found incredibly helpful and motivating (in a sense?), was the peer collaboration. This was the first time I had the opportunity to work on full projects in a duo at JMU, and I found it to be a very fun way to learn web dev.

While I wish I had stayed on top of assignments more this semester, thank you Dr. Stewart for being one of the most understanding and welcoming professors I've had at JMU.
